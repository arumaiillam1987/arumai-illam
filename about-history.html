🌿 History of Arumai Illam

Arumai Illam was founded on 27th July 1987 in the village of Thittai near Sirkali, Tamil Nadu. What began as a small initiative driven by compassion has grown into a dedicated charitable organization serving vulnerable communities with dignity and care.

Guided by the values of love, service, and social responsibility, Arumai Illam has supported the elderly, differently-abled individuals, orphaned children, and those abandoned by society.

📌 Key Milestones in Our Journey

🏁 1987 – Foundation & Registration

Registered under the Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act (Reg. No. 15/1987).

👂 Early Years – Special School Established

Started a school for children with hearing and speech disabilities.

❌ 2012 – Closure of Special School

Closed due to resource constraints and changing needs.

🧒 2012 – Integrated Care Home

Launched a home for both orphaned children and senior citizens, promoting intergenerational care.

🤝 2012–2025 – Government Support

Received grants from the Department of Social Welfare, Government of Tamil Nadu.

🔁 2021 – Community-Based Model

Shifted focus towards community-based care; orphanage component closed.

⚠️ 2025 – Grant Discontinued

Government grant stopped; continued services through alternative support.

🌱 Our Work Today

🏠 Home for Senior Citizens and Destitute

  • Safe and clean shelter
  • Nutritious meals
  • Medical care and assistance
  • Emotional and social support

🧠 School of Hope for Intellectually Disabled Children

  • Basic education
  • Life skills training
  • Therapeutic and emotional care

💖 Our Legacy & Mission

With over 35 years of service, Arumai Illam continues to stand firm in its commitment to compassion, dignity, and care for the most vulnerable.

Despite challenges, including the loss of government funding, our mission remains unchanged — to provide care, restore dignity, and bring hope to those who need it most.

“Even without support, our commitment continues — because care and compassion cannot be discontinued.”
